Kondapalli Fort

Kondapalli Fort, also known as Kondapalli Kota, is a 14th-century hill fortress located near Vijayawada in Andhra Pradesh. Known for its historic importance and panoramic views, it also serves as a gateway to Kondapalli village, which is famous for its wooden toys.

History

Built by Prolaya Vema Reddy of the Reddy dynasty in the 14th century, the fort later came under the rule of the Gajapati Kingdom, the Qutb Shahis, and the British.

Highlights

The fort has a massive three-story tower, deep reservoirs, and beautiful panoramic views of the surrounding hills. A walk through the ruins offers a glimpse into its past glory.

Travel Tips

  • Open from 10 AM to 5 PM
  • Entry Fee: ₹20 per person (subject to change)
  • Wear good walking shoes — terrain is uneven
