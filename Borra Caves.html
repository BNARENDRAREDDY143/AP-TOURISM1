Borra Caves

Located in the Ananthagiri Hills of the Araku Valley, Borra Caves are famous for their impressive stalactites and stalagmites, formed over millions of years and illuminated with vibrant lights.

History

Discovered by British geologist William King in 1807, these limestone caves are believed to be over a million years old and are associated with many local legends.

Highlights

Natural rock formations, Shiva Lingam, bat colonies, and panoramic views of the Eastern Ghats.
