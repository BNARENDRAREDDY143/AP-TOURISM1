Srikalahasti

Known as the 'Kashi of the South', this ancient Shiva temple is famous for its Rahu-Ketu poojas and is situated near Tirupati.

History

The temple is believed to be over 2000 years old and is mentioned in various ancient Hindu texts. It was rebuilt in the 12th century by King Rajendra Chola.

Highlights

Vayu Lingam (Air element), Rahu-Ketu pooja, temple carvings, and nearby Swarnamukhi river.
