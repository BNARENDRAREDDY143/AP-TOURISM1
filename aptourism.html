Sacred Temples

Tirupati

The spiritual capital of Andhra Pradesh, home to the famous Sri Venkateswara Temple atop Tirumala Hills, attracting millions of devotees annually.

History

Tirupati dates back to 300 AD, built by Tamil king Thondaimaan. The temple has been a pilgrimage site for centuries, mentioned in ancient texts like the Puranas.

Highlights

Brahmotsavam festival (September), Talakona Waterfalls (highest in AP), Sri Govindaraja Swamy Temple, Srivari Mettu trekking path

Accommodation

  • Marasa Sarovar Premiere (₹5,000-₹7,000) - Luxury with temple view
  • Hotel Pai Viceroy (₹2,500-₹3,500) - Mid-range near temple
  • TTD Choultries (₹100-₹500) - Budget pilgrim accommodation

Srisailam

A sacred temple town in the Nallamala Hills, home to one of the 12 Jyotirlingas of Lord Shiva and one of the 18 Shakti Peethas.

History

Linked to the 12th-century Mallikarjuna Jyotirlinga Temple, one of Lord Shiva's 12 sacred abodes. The site has been mentioned in ancient scriptures like the Skanda Purana.

Highlights

Srisailam Dam (one of India's largest), Srisailam Tiger Reserve (project tiger site), Patala Ganga ropeway, Akkamahadevi Caves

Accommodation

  • Haritha Hotel Srisailam (₹1,800-₹2,500) - Government-run with good facilities
  • Srisailam Devasthanam Cottages (₹1,000-₹1,500) - Basic pilgrim accommodation
  • Private Lodges (₹700-₹1,200) - Budget options near temple

Kanaka Durga Temple

A major Shakti Peetha located atop Indrakeeladri Hill overlooking the Krishna River, especially vibrant during Navratri.

History

Dates back to the 8th century, with legends tied to Goddess Durga slaying the demon Mahishasura. The temple was expanded by various dynasties including the Vijayanagara kings.

Highlights

Navratri celebrations (grandest in October), Prakasam Barrage (iconic landmark), Krishna River boating, Undavalli Caves nearby

Accommodation

  • Novotel Vijayawada (₹5,000-₹7,000) - 5-star with river view
  • Hotel Marg Krishnaaya (₹2,000-₹3,000) - Mid-range near temple
  • TTD Guest Houses (₹500-₹1,000) - Budget pilgrim stays

Misty Valleys

Araku Valley

A picturesque valley in the Eastern Ghats known for its coffee plantations, waterfalls, and rich tribal culture, located 120km from Visakhapatnam.

History

Home to various tribal communities for centuries, coffee plantations were introduced during British rule. The scenic train route was completed in the 1960s.

Highlights

Borra Caves (million-year-old limestone caves), Katiki Falls, Tribal Museum, Coffee Museum, Padmapuram Gardens, scenic train journey

Accommodation

  • Haritha Valley Resort (₹2,000-₹3,000) - Government-run with great views
  • Jungle Bells Nature Camp (₹1,500-₹2,500) - Forest department cottages
  • Private Homestays (₹800-₹1,500) - Experience tribal hospitality

Lambasingi

Known as the "Kashmir of Andhra Pradesh" for its cool climate and misty winters, with temperatures sometimes dropping below 0°C.

History

Gained prominence during British rule for its cool climate and tea plantations. The area is inhabited by tribal communities who have lived here for generations.

Highlights

Kothapalli Waterfalls (seasonal), coffee and tea plantations, misty landscapes (best Nov-Feb), Thajangi Reservoir, sunrise viewpoints

Accommodation

  • Haritha Hill Resort (₹1,800-₹2,500) - Basic government accommodation
  • Camping Sites (₹1,000-₹1,500) - Experience winter camping
  • Local Homestays (₹700-₹1,200) - Simple tribal-style stays

Horsley Hills

A charming hill station in the Chittoor district, named after British officer W.D. Horsley who established his summer residence here.

History

Developed as a summer retreat during British colonial rule. The governor's bungalow built in 1870 still stands as a heritage property.

Highlights

Kalyani viewpoint, Horsley Hills Zoo, eucalyptus trees, wind rocks, pine forests, and pleasant climate year-round

Accommodation

  • Haritha Hill Resort (₹2,000-₹3,000) - Well-maintained with great views
  • Private Cottages (₹1,500-₹2,500) - Cozy mountain stays
  • AP Tourism Guest House (₹1,200-₹1,800) - Basic but comfortable

Golden Beaches

Visakhapatnam

The "City of Destiny" known for its beautiful beaches, scenic landscapes, and as the financial capital of Andhra Pradesh.

History

Founded by Kulotunga Chola in the 11th-12th centuries AD, named after the Hindu god Visakha. Developed as a major port during British rule.

Highlights

RK Beach (main beach), Rushikonda (blue flag beach), Kailasagiri Hill (ropeway available), INS Kursura Submarine Museum, Yarada Beach, Dolphin's Nose

Accommodation

  • Novotel Visakhapatnam (₹6,000-₹8,000) - Beachfront luxury
  • The Gateway Hotel (₹3,000-₹4,500) - Sea-facing rooms
  • Haritha Beach Resort (₹1,200-₹2,000) - Budget beach stay

Suryalanka Beach

A serene 9km long beach known for its golden sands and calm waters, ideal for swimming and water sports, located near Bapatla.

History

Has been a local retreat for decades, gaining popularity as a serene coastal spot near Guntur. The beach is relatively undeveloped compared to other tourist beaches.

Highlights

Sunrise views (best from the lighthouse), beach volleyball, jet skiing, nearby Bhavani Island (in Vijayawada), Uppalapadu Bird Sanctuary

Accommodation

  • Haritha Beach Resort (₹1,800-₹2,500) - Beachfront cottages
  • Surya Resorts (₹1,200-₹1,800) - Mid-range with pool
  • Local Guest Houses (₹800-₹1,200) - Basic beachside stays

Mypad Beach

A pristine and relatively unexplored beach near Nellore, known for its natural beauty and peaceful atmosphere.

History

Historically a fishing village, now gaining attention for tourism. The beach remains largely undeveloped, preserving its natural charm.

Highlights

Dolphin sightings (seasonal), beach camping, fishing village visits, Pulicat Lake nearby (second largest brackish water lake in India)

Accommodation

  • Beachside Cottages (₹1,500-₹2,500) - Basic but scenic
  • Hotels in Nellore (₹1,000-₹2,000) - 30km from beach
  • Camping (₹500-₹1,000) - With local operators

Historical Marvels

Amaravati

An ancient Buddhist center and capital of the Satavahana dynasty with the Great Stupa, now the planned capital city of Andhra Pradesh.

History

Capital of the Satavahana dynasty (2nd century BCE - 3rd century CE), an important Buddhist center. The stupa was built during Ashoka's reign and was one of the largest in India.

Highlights

Amaravati Stupa ruins, Dhyana Buddha Statue (125ft tall), Archaeological Museum, Krishna River views, upcoming capital city infrastructure

Accommodation

  • Capital O Hotels (₹2,000-₹3,000) - Modern amenities
  • Haritha Hotel (₹1,200-₹1,800) - Government-run
  • Local Guest Houses (₹800-₹1,200) - Budget options

Lepakshi

A 16th-century Vijayanagara Empire marvel with exquisite architecture, frescoes, and the famous hanging pillar.

History

Built during the Vijayanagara Empire under King Achutaraya, the temple is dedicated to Veerabhadra. The hanging pillar is an engineering marvel that baffles visitors.

Highlights

Monolithic Nandi statue (largest in India), hanging pillar (doesn't rest on ground), frescoes (best preserved in India), UNESCO tentative site, giant footprint legend

Accommodation

  • Haritha Hotel Lepakshi (₹1,500-₹2,000) - Near temple complex
  • Local Guest Houses (₹800-₹1,200) - Basic facilities
  • Hotels in Hindupur (₹1,000-₹1,800) - 15km from site

Gandikota Fort

The "Grand Canyon of India" with stunning Pennar River gorge views, featuring a massive 13th-century fort complex.

History

Built in the 13th century by the Pemmasani Nayaks, strategically located on the Pennar River. Served as an important stronghold for various dynasties.

Highlights

Fort ruins (spread over 10 sq km), mosque, Madhavaraya temple, canyon views (best at sunrise/sunset), adventure activities (rappelling, camping)

Accommodation

  • Haritha Hotel Gandikota (₹1,800-₹2,500) - Canyon view rooms
  • Camping Sites (₹1,000-₹1,500) - Organized by AP Tourism
  • Guest Houses in Jammalamadugu (₹800-₹1,200) - 15km from fort
