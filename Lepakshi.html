Lepakshi

Lepakshi, located in Andhra Pradesh, is renowned for its exquisite Vijayanagara architecture, especially the Veerabhadra Temple, which features stunning murals and a legendary hanging pillar.

History

The temple was built in the 16th century by the brothers Viranna and Virupanna, who were governors under the Vijayanagara king Achutaraya.

Highlights

Veerabhadra Temple, Hanging Pillar, Nandi Statue, frescoes, sculptures, and mythology related to Ramayana.

Travel Tips

  • Open from 6 AM to 6 PM daily
  • Best visited between October and February
  • Combine with a day trip to Hindupur or Penukonda
